1. Industry overview: wedding video & livestream

Over the last three years, wedding video has rapidly evolved from "a single 30-minute documentary cut" into a triple bundle of same-day-edit + wedding livestream + wedding MV. Couples now expect:

  • A same-day-edit (30s–60s) within 2 hours of the ceremony
  • A wedding livestream for remote family and friends
  • A wedding MV (3–5 minutes, cinematic) within one week
  • A long-form documentary cut (15–30 minutes) within one month

This pushes wedding video teams to upgrade their pipeline, capacity and brand for an internet-first market — exactly the gap a brand domain like wedding-tv.cn is built for.

3. Wedding MV & same-day-edit: style and pricing

Mainstream wedding MV styles in 2026:

  • Cinematic film: 4K, 24fps, anamorphic, moody grade
  • Cream tone: high-key, low-saturation, warm white
  • Retro Hong Kong: grain, teal-orange, 4:3
  • Documentary natural: handheld, ambient light, minimal grade

4. Wedding videography pricing 2026

Typical wedding videography rates (per day, USD):

  • Single-cam shoot: $260 – $500
  • Dual-cam photo + video: $650 – $1,200
  • Three-cam + crane: $1,300 – $2,600
  • Add wedding livestream: +$300 – $900
  • Add same-day-edit delivery: +$120 – $220

5. Wedding SaaS & chain-brand upgrade

Wedding chains and planning platforms increasingly run on wedding SaaS for:

  • Lead CRM (sources: TikTok, Instagram, local listings)
  • Calendar & videographer scheduling
  • Client gallery delivery + livestream link generation
  • Billing & revenue split

This kind of product needs a short, memorable, industry-specific brand domain. wedding-tv.cn combines the universal industry word "wedding" with "tv" — instantly mapped to video and livestream — a strong narrative fit for wedding SaaS.

6. Wedding brand naming & domain strategy

Common naming patterns for wedding video / livestream / SaaS brands:

  1. Industry + medium: wedding + tv / live / film / mv (strong category signal)
  2. Emotional concept + TLD: moment / forever / vow (emotional pull)
